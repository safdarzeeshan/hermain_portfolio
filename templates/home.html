Connector App



Initial Concept


Through stakeholder interviews, brainstorming and prototype testing, I helped the team in designing an app for contractors who wanted to complete projects safely, on schedule and in budget. The features explored in the wireframes included instant feedback and rating service, an excavation relevant educational module, calendar module and a module to request locate service. The layout was designed in a manner so that the app can be used in the excavation field as each feature is one click away.







Final Result


My team and correspondents from Union Gas worked together to define risks, schedule the development, identify the costs and prepare final layout. Linked with the ON1CALL website, now the contractors can see the locators associated with their project and give their feedback on their service. New features allow infrastructure owners to push messages on the live feed to connect with contractors across Ontario.

ED Support Tool


About Eating Disorders


Eating disorders are mental disorders which give individuals a false sense of being in control of their body image by controlling their food intake. They are not just about food. They are often a way to cope with difficult problems or regain a sense of control. With a large population experiencing symptoms and lack of support available, eating disorders have become a growing concern in Canada.

We worked with Rose McDonald, caregiver at Danielle’s Place, to understand the challenges faced by families having an eating disorder patient. She explained about the difficulties in finding support, disruption in family environments and unusual child growth. She identified the need of an online platform which can help connect families with each other and with caregivers.


Concept/Journey Map





Current Platform Prototype


After extensive user experience testing, we designed an online platform that encourages parents to share their stories with other families and various caregivers. With a clean interface the parents will be able to access each feature instantly during emergency situations. They will be able to record the behavior of their children and transfer this history to a doctor so that more informed decision can be taken on a child’s progress on his/her road to recovery.







Dishwasher for elderly


As an independent project I started investigating the challenges faced by elderly while operating current dishwashers. After several interviews and task analysis, I found that they struggle in bending, carrying dishes to the dishwasher and often forget using soap. Also placement and removal of dishes causes problem.

The dishwasher bot is a conceptual design of a dishwasher that targets the challenges described above.


Mind mapping





Storyboarding


The dishwasher bot is essentially a small robot that can be controlled easily using a joystick. Using local wifi/bluetooth it can be brought closer to a user's position. To avoid the effort in bending to place the dishes, the dishwasher bot has been provided the ability to adjust it's height. By adding flexible water hose and wires this design can become a reality. AFO Design Concept


People experiencing foot drop often use an AFO brace that is worn on the lower leg and foot to support the ankle, hold the foot and ankle in the correct position and correct foot drop. While pursuing my master's program we worked with a client to understand the problems faced by patients using AFO braces. They identified the difficulties in storing it, carrying it to other places and mouting it on their ankles. Our aim was to reduce the size and weight of an AFO brace to make it portable and eliminate it's visibility from outside.

Prototypes created


Using everyday material, we created low budget prototypes to test their functionality. Each prototype was an improvement over the predecessor interms of reduced weight and improved functionality. Prototype 1 is designed to be worn over the shoe while prototype 2 provides similar funtion but can be hidden beneath the clothes.



Opportunities in fracking


Representatives from Chemical Industy Association of Canada approached our team with a challenge of reducing the environmental damages caused by shale gas fracking process. They wanted us to identify areas that should be explored to acheive certain safety standards. Accepted as the best practice, we started our process by identifying and interviewing the stakeholders associated with fracking. These included extractors, consumers, community members and environmental protection agencies.

The areas we identified as potential for exploration included water contamination and consumption by nearby communities, waste fracking fluid open decomposition and wasteage of water on each well. To illustrate them we sketched certain product ideas that can be fabricated.



Spiral Heat-exchanger design



The present shell and tube heat exchangers require larger space and continuous maintenance to ensure efficient heat transfer. A spiral plate heat exchanger, on the other hand, provides similar heat transfer with minimum maintenance and construction cost. This project explores the design of a spiral plate heat exchanger for heating Palm oil using water in an oil refinery.


The image below shows the exploded veiw of the spiral heat exchanger assembly. It consists of an outer shell that houses the spiral plates. This shell is covered at the ends using flanges and is supported on a frame.


The image below shows the passages that form due to the coiling of two steel plates around each other. These passages are then used for flowing a hot and colder fluid across each other.
